Cell Therapy (PRP)

Awakening the Body's Own
Healing Power from Within

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy is a
cutting-edge regenerative treatment that concentrates
the growth factors responsible for
healing and tissue repair
from the patient's own blood,
then delivers them directly to the site of
damaged ligaments, tendons, or cartilage.

effect 01

effect 02

Root-Cause Treatment

PRP actively regenerates and strengthens the weakened tissue itself.

Powerful Regeneration

Indication

Ideal Candidates

"Particularly effective for chronic pain that is slow to heal or frequently recurs"

PRP is recommended for patients with chronic musculoskeletal conditions who have experienced limited response to conventional physical therapy or pharmacotherapy, or who have suffered recurrence following corticosteroid injections.

Key Indications

  • Elbow Pain: Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow), Medial Epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow)
  • Knee Pain: Early-to-Moderate Osteoarthritis, Cartilage Damage
  • Tendon & Ligament Disorders: Chronic Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy (Shoulder), Achilles Tendinopathy, Wrist/Ankle Tenosynovitis, and more
Process

Treatment Process

"From blood draw to injection — completed in approximately 30–60 minutes on the same day"

No hospitalization or complex anesthesia required. Performed entirely on an outpatient basis.

  • STEP 1. Blood Draw A small volume of blood (approximately 20–30 cc) is drawn from the patient's arm.
  • STEP 2. Centrifugation & Concentration Using a specialized processing kit and centrifuge, high-concentration PRP is isolated from the blood sample. (Approximately 15 minutes)
  • STEP 3. Image-Guided Injection Under high-resolution real-time ultrasound guidance, the concentrated PRP is precisely injected into the target site.
Post Care

Post-Procedure Care

"Essential guidance to maximize treatment effect and support rapid recovery"

PRP is a tissue regeneration treatment — not a conventional painkiller. Proper post-procedure care and synergy treatments significantly influence the outcome.

  • Initial Response (Normal Healing Process): A temporary increase in soreness or a burning sensation may occur for 2–3 days following the procedure. This is a normal and expected sign that tissue repair is actively underway.
  • Rest & Activity Restriction: For approximately one week following the procedure, avoid strenuous exercise or overuse of the treated area and allow adequate rest to support optimal healing.
Synergy

Maximizing Results Through Combination Therapy

To enhance PRP's regenerative effect and shorten recovery time, the following complementary treatments may be recommended by our physician for a powerful synergistic outcome.

  • Accelerating Regeneration: ESWT (Extracorporeal Shock Wave Therapy) and HILT (High-Intensity Laser Therapy) provide additional cellular stimulation to accelerate the pace of tissue repair.
  • Pain Management: Combining Cryotherapy immediately post-procedure effectively reduces acute pain and swelling during the initial healing phase.
  • Functional Recovery: Combining specialized physical therapy and manual therapy to restore the functional capacity of regenerated tissue has been shown to significantly reduce the rate of recurrence.
